Blue Labyrinths

Blue Labyrinths is an ongoing cyanotype project exploring the politics of the archive and the future afterlives of digital objects.

Using an analog photographic process, historical fragments are removed from online repositories and reorganized into new constellations that blur archaeology, memory, authorship, and intellectual property.

The work is concerned with disintegration—material, historical, and digital—and with the strange temporalities that emerge when ancient objects circulate through contemporary technologies.

The Origin of Desire

The Origin of Desire draws on Aristophanes’ account of desire in Plato’s Symposium together with Freud’s concepts of Eros and the death drive. Through large-scale cyanotypes, the series explores desire as an impulse toward union that is inseparable from loss, fragmentation, and mortality.

About

Valerie Trachsler is an American interdisciplinary artist based in Madrid, Spain. Working across photography, cyanotype, and installation, her practice investigates archives, material memory, and the movement of images between digital and physical worlds.

Drawing on classical antiquity, reception studies, hauntology, psychoanalysis, and disintegration aesthetics, she creates works that juxtapose ancient artifacts with contemporary systems of reproduction and preservation.

She holds a degree in History and Classical Philology from CUNY Brooklyn College in New York City.
